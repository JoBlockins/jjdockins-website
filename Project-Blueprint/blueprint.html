About Project Blueprint

Project Blueprint is a career-exploration initiative I created as I began seriously looking at colleges and thinking about my future. With the rising cost of attending a four-year university, I realized how important it is to have a clearer understanding of what careers actually look like—and which ones might genuinely interest me before committing to a major or long-term path.

To do this, I reach out to friends, family, mentors, and people throughout my community to learn about their professional journeys. Each conversation is guided by a consistent set of 50 in-depth questions that explore how they discovered their field, what their day-to-day looks like, the challenges they've faced, and the advice they would share with someone starting out.

Every interview becomes its own "blueprint"—a real, honest snapshot of a person's career path. The project shows that careers are rarely linear, that opportunities can appear unexpectedly, and that success is built from experience, resilience, and self-discovery.

The goal of Project Blueprint is simple: to gather meaningful insights that help me—and anyone following along—better understand career options, explore new interests, and make smarter decisions about education and the future.

Interested in Project Blueprint?

If this project resonates with you and you're open to sharing your career journey, I'd love to connect. Project Blueprint is built on the generosity of people willing to reflect on their experiences, and every story adds meaningful insight.

Whether you're early in your career, well established, changing fields, or retired, your perspective can help students better understand the many paths a career can take.

If you'd like to participate or learn more, you can:

  • Complete the 50-question career questionnaire
  • Reach out directly to ask questions or schedule a conversation

Your experience could help shape someone's future, and I'd be grateful to include your story in Project Blueprint.
