About Me

Hi, my name is Josiah Dockins, and I'm a junior at Bedford High School in Bedford, New Hampshire (Class of 2027). I row year-round for Amoskeag Rowing Club and the Bedford High School Rowing Team, and I'm working toward competing at the highest level I can in college. Rowing has become the foundation of my daily life. It's where I've learned discipline, consistency, and how much I enjoy pushing myself to improve every day.

I'm committed to developing as an athlete, student, and a general person. Academically, I challenge myself with one of the most rigorous course loads available at my school, including IB and advanced math and science classes. I take pride in being someone who is trustworthy, responsible, and organized, the kind of person who manages school, training, and long-term goals without losing focus or motivation.

On the rowing side, I'm a 6'0", 160-lb lightweight rower with a strong drive to get faster. I train year-round, and I'm steadily improving my erg numbers and strength as I target major goals like pushing my 2k under 6:30. I care a lot about technique, teamwork, and showing up every day ready to become 1% better. I'm coachable, competitive, and I take feedback seriously. I not only want to be the best possible rower myself, but also work to uplift my team on and off the water.

I'm also very interested in pursuing business, finance, or economics in college, and I'm exploring schools where I can grow in the classroom, contribute to a team culture, and be surrounded by other driven student-athletes.

Why I Row

Brief Introduction

I'm a high school rower who trains year-round and takes pride in being consistent and disciplined in my approach to the sport. Rowing has become an important part of my life, not just physically but mentally as well, and it has shaped how I set goals, manage my time, and handle challenges. I value being part of a team environment that emphasizes accountability, effort, and continuous improvement.

Why I Like Rowing

I like rowing because it unifies people all working towards the same goal with absolute focus. When I'm on the water, there's no technology or outside distractions, it's just the boat, and the effort I'm putting in. I enjoy the simplicity of trying to make the boat move as fast and efficiently as possible, and I find that focus both challenging and rewarding. Rowing also teaches patience and discipline, since improvement comes from showing up and doing the work consistently.

Why I Like the Erg

For me, erging is one of the most fascinating parts of rowing. The erg is simply a tool that converts effort into results and that is why I appreciate it so much. The people who put their heads down and workout consistently for months will be successful and make incredible fitness gains. That simplicity makes erging one of my favorite aspects of training.

Why Rowing in College Is Important to Me

Rowing in college is important to me because I want to keep pushing myself in an environment that values hard work, discipline, and commitment. I'm excited by the challenge of balancing demanding academics with a competitive athletic program, and I want to be part of a team that holds itself to a high standard. College rowing represents an opportunity for me to continue growing as an athlete while also developing the habits and mindset that will carry over into the rest of my life.
